Target Case Study

David Wang
Kyle Komo
Paul LaRue

Target business model

  • Expect more pay less
  • Focus on overall shopping experience
    • Emphasizing store decor to give the right shopping ambience
  • Target demographic
    • College educated
    • More affluent than typical wal-mart shopper
    • Upper middle class

Main competitors

Each retailer have a different strategy and customer base, thus comparable stores are hard to identify.

Most investment analysts agree on these companies

  • Wal-Mart
  • Costco
  • Store format similar to Target
  • Focus on price reduction to gain market share
  • key strategy is price cutting
  • Competes directly with Target's core customer base
  • Sells customers at cost of goods plus cost of overhead in exchange for them paying a membership fee
  • key strategy is price cutting

Growth for retail companies

Stems from two main sources

  • Creation of new stores
    • Costly, necessary to gain access to new markets
  • Organic growth from existing stores
    • Renovation or upgrade

Capital budgeting process

  • Project goes through capital expenditure committee
    • Three executive VPs, president, and CEO
    • Meets once a month
    • Reviews up to 15 proposals per meeting
  • Responsible for project ranging from $100,000 to 50 million
  • Project greater than 50 million requires approval from the board of directors
  • Target real estate managers seeks out potential locations and submits project proposals

Growth objective

  • Primary goal is to open 100 new stores per year
  • Real estate managers must find markets that have
    • population growth
    • Income level meeting Target demographic

Critique of capital budgeting process

Strengths

  • Target operates in low margin market, quantitative methods alone such as NPV and IRR, do not show qualtative factors, such as brand awareness, market competitiveness, market sensitivity
  • Target's dashboard system is thus a good overview for making project decisions

Critique of capital budgeting process

Weaknesses

  • Long planning period before a projects can be submitted,
    • 12 to 24 months
    • Time sensitivity opportunites may be missed
  • Lack of diversity in CEC
    • Consists only of high level officers
    • Lacks insight from other levels of the company

Potential Projects

  • All 5 CPRs have positive NPV
  • Four new openings
    • Gopher Place
    • Whalen Court
    • The Barn
    • Goldie's Square
  • One remodel
    • Stadium Remodel

1. Stadium remodel

Stadium remodel

  • Inital investment of $17 million
  • Meets Target's demographics
    • Average income $65,931, with 42% atended a 4 year college
  • Succesful store in existing market
  • Estimated 10-17% boost in sales after remodeling
  • Remodeling maintains brand image at location with significant target demographic
  • Risk Level: Low
    • 10% decrease in sales would result in $7.8 million in loss
    • Scenario is highly unlikely since current decline is only 0.9% per year

2. Gopher Place

2. Gopher Place

  • Inital investment of $23 million
  • Meets Target's demographics
    • Average income $56,400
    • High population growth of 27%
  • Important in maintaining local market share
    • Wal-Mart is expected to open two new stores in the area
  • Acceptable level of store sensitivity
    • 5.3% decrease in sale will still meet R&P projection
  • Risk Level: Low
    • Projected 8% cannibalization

3. The Barn

3. The Barn

  • Low investment of $13 million
  • Entry into new market
  • Low level of store sensitivity
    • 10% decrease in sale will only result in 4 million loss
  • Risk Level: Medium
    • Average income level does not meet Target's core demographic

4. Whalen Court

4. Whalen Court

  • Inital investment of $119 million
    • Must be approved by board of directors
  • Meets Target's demographics
    • Average income $48,500, with 45% atended a 4 year college
  • High potential payoff
  • Risk Level: High
    • Low population growth of 3%
    • Cannibalization of 5%
    • High investment, high risk
    • Property is a lease

5. Goldie's Square

5. Goldie's Square

  • Inital investment of $23.9 million
  • Meets Target's demographics
    • Average income $56,000, with 24% atended a 4 year college
    • High population growth of 16%
  • Risk Level: High
    • Low NPV
    • All factors are below prototype
    • 10% decline in sales puts NPV in the negatives
    • Reject project if possible
